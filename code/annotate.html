Introduction

We are a research group conducting a study on the subjectivity of human skin tone annotations. In this task, you will be asked to annotate your skin tone and the skin tone of others by ranking an icon and images (respectively) on a scale using the interface we provide. Below is a brief overview of the tasks in this experiment:

  • We will first collect your consent for participation and then present a brief tutorial on how to use the tool.
  • You will then be asked to identify your own skin tone along the given scale.
  • Following this, you will be presented with a series of six (6) images of human subjects.
  • For each item, you will be asked to place it onto the scale based on the the human subjectâ€™s overall skin tone (predominant complexion) by first finding the lightest tone where the subject may be placed on the scale followed by finding the darkest tone where the subject can be placed.

For this offline recruitment of the experiment, you will be paid via a $10 gift card. Make sure to fill out this form: https://forms.gle/PLEASE_MAKE_YOUR_OWN_FORM

Note: Some images in this task are drawn from medical datasets and may depict skin infections. These images may be unsettling and cause discomfort. If you can no longer complete the task please exit the study using the "Exit" button at the bottom left of the page.

Note: You may only participate in this study once. There is no compensation for duplicate submissions on this HIT after your first.

Annotation Tutorial

In this training example, we will guide you through the process of annotating the skin tone of a single example. In this example case, we will be placing a photo of a person on a scale based on your estimate of their predominant skin tone. Since there is always some uncertainty in the apparent skin tone of a person in a photo (lighting, clarity of photo, etc.), we will instead annotate the picture by finding the upper and lower bounds of the image subject's skin tone using skin tone color swatches as reference. Example images of people will also be placed along the scale for you to compare with the given image.

Note: Please disable any display brightness/color adjustment software, remove any color-adjusting screen protectors and adjust the brightness level to what is comfortable for you to clearly see the images and scale. These measures will ensure a more consistent environment for viewing the skin tone of image subjects.

Step 1: Find lower tone bound

For this step we're trying to find the lower bound of the image subjectâ€™s skin tone. This means we want to find out the darkest position the person can be placed on the scale. Using the slider below, compare the current photo against the existing skin tone types on the scale.

Scroll the slider handle from left to right. Compare the current photo to the skin tone swatches on its left and right as you're sliding the handle. Use the examples underneath the slider to quickly skip over areas

Position the slider so that you're confident the photo to the left shows someone who is definitely darker, while the photo to the right is lighter (can be similar in skin tone or lighter).

Click "Confirm" to confirm your pick and fix the lower bound.

Step 2: Find upper skin tone range

Great! Now it's time to find the upper bound. This means we want to find out the lightest place the person can be placed on the scale. Notice that the position for the lower bound (that we just annotated) has been fixed in place. Like before, move the slider handle for the upper bound to compare the current photo against existing skin tone swatches.

This time, position the slider handle so that the photo to the right shows an example of a skin tone that is lighter than the image subject, while the example of the skin tone to the left is darker (can be similar skin tone or darker).

The blue shaded range indicates the skin tone range of the current photo. The photos that are in this range should be a similar skin tone to the current photo (not drastically lighter or darker).

Once you're done, click on "Done" to check your answer and progress onto the main task.

Annotate Your Own Skin Tone

You have successfully completed the tutorial, we now ask for you annotate your own skin tone. Please complete this task carefully because this will also be collected as a part of the research study. Simply follow the steps outlined in the tutorial (Step 1: Find lower bound for skin tone and Step 2: Find upper bound for skin tone) and place the anonymous profile icon along the scale.

Instructions for Evaluating Your Skin Tone
  • Use the tone from the inside of your arm for a consistent tone not varied by tanning
  • Be in a well-lit area (preferably with natural lighting)
  • To improve accuracy, please use your apparent in-person skin tone rather than a photo of yourself
  • Do not consult with other individuals when annotating your skin tone
